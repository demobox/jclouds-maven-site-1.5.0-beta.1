43  /**
44   * The ResourceAllocationSettingData class represents settings specifically
45   * related to an allocated resource that are outside the scope of the CIM class
46   * typically used to represent the resource itself.
47   *
48   * These settings include information specific to the allocation that may not
49   * be visible to the consumer of the resource itself. For example, a virtual
50   * processor may look like a 2 GHz processor to the consumer (virtual computer
51   * system), however the virtualization system may use time-slicing to schedule
52   * the the virtual processor to only allow it to use 1 GHz.
53   *

55   * @author grkvlt@apache.org
56   * @see <a href="http://schemas.dmtf.org/wbem/wscim/1/cim-schema/2.22.0/CIM_ResourceAllocationSettingData.xsd">CIM_ResourceAllocationSettingData</a>
